Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I wanted to look at different factors that could affect the bike trip duration. The main focus was on the following factors: hour, age, user type, user gender, and enrolling in the "bike share for all" program.

Dataset Overview¶

The dataset is taken from https://www.fordgobike.com/system-data and represents trips taken by members of the service for month of February of 2019. The data consists of info about trips taken by service's members, their types, their age, their gender, stations of starting and ending trips, duration of trips etc..The column "bike_share_for_all_trip" tracks members who are enrolled in the Bike Share for All program for low-income residents.

Trip Duration Distribution¶

  • Trip Duration in the dataset take on a very large range of values, from about 61 sec at the lowest, to about 84548 sec at the highest, with a lot of trips on the range of [400, 600] sec, and few the trips took higher than 2000 sec.

  • When plotted on a log-scale, the Trip Duration distribution looks roughly unimodal, with one peak between [400, 600] sec.

Hours Duration Distribution¶

  • The number of trips during hours of going and coming back from work (rush hours) is large.

  • The hour distribution looks roughly bimodal, with one peak between 9 and 10 am, and a second peak between 5 and 6 pm.

  • Interestingly, there's a steady frequency pattern in the No. of trips between the two peaks (work hours), rather than the left and right side of the plot.

User Type Vs. Trip Duration¶

Customer users tend to ride the bikes in higher duration than subscribers. This might be because the customer users ride the bikes for irregular commutes while the subscriber users might use the bikes for school or work.

Trip Duration Across User Type and Hour¶

  • For Customer types, the hours ranges of 11-15 have the longest trip duration, this is maybe due to the customer users might be tourists that use the bikes for traveling around town.

  • For subscriber types, the hours ranges of 0-3, 7-11, and 11-7 have the longest trip duration, this is maybe due to The subscriber users ride the bikes for regular commutes (work, school, etc.).

  • My biggest surprise was that the second longest trip duration in subscriber type belongs to the hours ranges of 0-3. I would have thought that the longest trip duration for Subscribers would be 7-11 or 11-7. My assumption is maybe the hours ranges of 0-3 would suitable for Cycle sport.

Conclusion¶

  • In Feb 2019, the average trip duration that the users took was around 10 minutes. The trips were mostly in rush hours (9-10 am and 5-6 pm.) and most trips were taken by subscriber males who were not members of GoBike. Also, the age group with the most trips was between 30-40 years old.

  • The Customers users took longer duration in their rides than Subscribers.

  • For Customer types, the hours ranges of 11-15 have the longest trip duration, this is maybe due to the customer users might be tourists that use the bikes for traveling around town.

  • For subscriber types, the hours ranges of 0-3, 7-11, and 11-7 have the longest trip duration, this is maybe due to The subscriber users ride the bikes for regular commutes (work, school, Cycling sport etc.)

  • The gender did not affect the average trip duation
